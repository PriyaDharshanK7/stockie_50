About the Stock Market

Gain deeper insight into the foundations, history, and growth of the Indian stock market to empower your investing journey.

Stock Market Basics

The stock market is a centralized place where shares of publicly listed companies are traded. In India, it plays a pivotal role in the economy, facilitating capital formation and giving individuals an opportunity to invest and grow their wealth.

A Brief History

1875

The Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) laid the foundation for formal stock trading in India.

1986

The BSE Sensex, a benchmark index, was introduced to track the performance of 30 well-established companies.

1992

The National Stock Exchange (NSE) was established, launching an electronic trading system that reduced transaction times and improved market efficiency.

2000

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) introduced new regulations to protect investors and ensure market transparency.

2008

The global financial crisis impacted the Indian stock market, leading to significant reforms and increased regulatory oversight.

Present Day

Rapid modernization, digital transformation, and regulatory frameworks continue to shape the Indian market, making it one of the fastest-growing globally.
